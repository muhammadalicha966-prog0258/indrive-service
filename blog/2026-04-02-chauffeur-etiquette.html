The Ultimate Guide to Chauffeur Etiquette

Published on April 2, 2026

Riding in a chauffeured vehicle is a distinct experience from hailing a taxi. It is designed to be seamless, perfectly relaxing, and strictly professional. Whether you are a first-time executive traveler or a seasoned diplomat, understanding basic etiquette ensures the best possible experience for both you and your driver.

Let the Chauffeur Open the Door

Your chauffeur is trained to handle your entry and exit. Wait for them to open the door for you. This applies to both entering and leaving the vehicle. It avoids any awkward timing and allows the chauffeur to ensure the street is clear before you step out.

Communicate Upfront

If you have specific preferences—such as a preferred temperature, a specific radio station, or the desire for complete silence—communicate this clearly at the beginning of the journey. Professional drivers appreciate direct instructions to tailor the environment to your liking.
